About the Show

Aging Cervantes professor Jose Quijano isn’t going down without a fight. When his family tries to move him to an assisted living facility, Jose escapes on his valiant tricycle steed in search of his long-lost love. Reality and fantasy blur in the Texas desert, as Jose dubs himself Don Quixote and embarks on a fantastic, death-defying journey to discover the truth of his past. Underscored by vibrant Tejano music, Quixote Nuevo is a humorous and heartfelt quest towards becoming the hero of your own story.

Featured Performers

Lakin Valdez

Role: Bruno/Young Quijano

"Born and raised in the extended family of El Teatro Campesino, he served as the company’s associate artistic director from 2000 to 2005. Mr. Valdez has written, directed, and performed in countless productions nationally, culminating in work that reflects the rich history and vibrant culture of the US Latino/Chicano community. He is the recipient of two National Performance Network Creation Fund awards and a National Endowment for the Arts Access to Excellence Award, among others." - Goodman Theatre

Sol Castillo

Role: Padre Perez

"Born into a performing family, it looked like I was destined to be in the Entertainment business. Luckily, I've been a professional Actor since the age of 10. Performed on stages all across the country as a speaker, Actor and musician. I've been on TV, in films and done practically every kind of live theatre. I am a proud Chicano who has marched all over California in the fight for social justice, equal rights and the representation of brown faces in Entertainment. In these posts, you will find my thoughts on Acting, Writing, Music, Activism and more." - Sol Castillo
